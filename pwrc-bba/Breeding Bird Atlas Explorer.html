Description:
Abstract:
The Breeding Bird Atlas (BBA) Explorer is a website and data management system that offers data from published BBAs and interim data from ongoing BBAs in the United States in a uniform system. It allows users to learn about the methods used in each atlas project, to get species and block information for each project, and to view the results by species, block, or region in a table or map format. Most descriptive information and tabular results are available through each Atlas Home page and maps are available through our BBA Species Map tool. There are over 30 state datasets, and a dozen county-level atlas datasets, contained in the repository, but it does not contain all of the atlas projects in North American. The database housing these geospatial datasets contains more information on request, mostly metadata about methods and more detailed effort and observational data that may be useful in research. See our contact information for further information.Additional breeding bird atlas datasets can be obtained through Bird Studies Canada, which holds all of the Canadian atlas datasets, and the Cornell Lab of Ornithology, which houses several recent or current atlas projects through their website.
Purpose:
The Breeding Bird Atlas (BBA) Explorer is a website and data management system that offers data from published BBAs and interim data from ongoing BBAs in the United States in a uniform system. Breeding bird atlases are avian distribution survey projects designed to cover large geographic areas using a grid based system and are generally organized at the state or provincial level.
Supplemental_Information:
We attempt to provide this information in multiple formats. First, we provide web-based mapping that does not require the user to have GIS at all. These maps are available at the main URI.
Next we have provided shapefiles in the past, however we intend to move towards other formats: ESRI file geodatabases, and Web mapping services.

Use_Constraints:
USGS Patuxent Wildlife Research Center's website and date of access should be cited in any use of this database (see Citation section).
Substantial use of any single atlas project's data in publication or reports should cite those individual projects as well (citation format on each relevant page); and we encourage users to seek out the print publications in this case as they provide more substantial information and interpretation of data and maps. For extensive use of atlas data in research publications, we welcome invitations of co-authorship, but it is not a requirement.
Data_Set_Credit:
Thousands of volunteers in atlas project in over 30 states and a dozen counties collected the data for each breeding bird atlas project. Individual project sponsors are listed here: <http://www.pwrc.usgs.gov/bba/index.cfm?fa=bba.Sponsors>. More details on each projects, and contact and source information for each (state or county) project is contained in the individual project datasets and metadata records. We compiled these datasets for use in a common system for access at USGS Patuxent Wildlife Research Center. Data compilers include Naoko Griffin, Allison Sussman, Anna Ott, Mark Wimer. The compiling of atlas datasets was supported primarily through funding from the National Biological Information Infrastructure, or NBII, Bird Conservation Node. Elizabeth Martin at NBII was central to the support of this database from 2001 to 2010.
